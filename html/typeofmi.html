Types of Multiple Intelligences

  1. Interpersonal
  2. Intrapersonal
  3. Logical
  4. Spatial
  5. Bodily-Kinesthetic
  6. Linguistic
  7. Musical
  8. Naturalist
  9. Existential
1. Interpersonal

Interpersonal intelligence is the ability to relate and understand other peoples’ emotions and intentions. These people are extroverts and like to work with others and are natural leaders.
2. Interpersonal

Intrapersonal intelligence deals with self awareness, individual character and expertise in one’s own emotions, strengths, weakness and goals. They are able to learn and get motivated through life’s experiences and failures.

3. Logical

Logical intelligence is about the ability to use reason, logic and numbers. They have a good concept of cause and effect about things. Logical intelligent people love mathematics and science and are sensitive to numerals.
4. Spatial

Spatial intelligence is dealing with the ability in visualizing and manipulating objects visually. Spatial people are interested in colors, space and design, strong visual memory and love drawing. 
5. Bodily-Kinesthetic

Bodily kinesthetic is the intelligence dealing with body movements and handle objects skillfully. Kinesthetic people have a good sense of balance and eye-hand coordination and make use of body language while expressing feelings and thoughts.
6. Linguistic

Linguistic intelligence refers to the ability to use words and language. Linguistic people have highly developed auditory skills and are highly capable of developing language skills.

7. Musical

People who have musical intelligence are sensitive to sounds, music, tones and rhythms. Such people are more likely to demonstrate strong abilities in pitch recognition, singing, and music playing or composing.

8. Naturalist

People having naturalist intelligence can relate to nature. They learn well by doing something outside the classroom or by doing something physically. They excel in subjects that require collecting and analyzing, or are closely related to nature.

9. Existential

Existential intelligence can be defined as the ability to be sensitive to, or have the capacity for, conceptualizing or tackling deeper or larger questions about human existence, such as the meaning of life, why are we born, why do we die, what is consciousness, or how did we get here.
